# Authoritarian-Era Presidential Speeches (South Korea, 1961-1992)

**File:** `authoritarian_speeches_sample.csv`
**Rows:** 600 speeches
**Size:** ~3.23 MB
**Source:** `nlp_corpora/data/president_speeches/president_speech_ko.csv`
(8,771-speech original, all 12 South Korean presidents 1948-2022).

This corpus is the official rhetoric of the three South Korean presidents
who governed the country during its authoritarian-developmental period:

- **Park Chung-hee** (박정희): from his 1961 coup through his assassination
  in October 1979 — the era of state-led industrialisation, Yusin
  constitutional autocracy, and Cold War alignment with the US and Japan.
- **Chun Doo-hwan** (전두환): from the 1980 Gwangju massacre through 1988
  — the Fifth Republic, military rule with managed economic growth.
- **Roh Tae-woo** (노태우): from June 1987's "democratic transition"
  declaration through 1992 — the early-democracy bridge years (Sixth
  Republic, Northern Diplomacy, Olympic-era opening).

Speeches dated **on or before 31 December 1992** are kept (Roh's term
formally ended 25 February 1993, but no Roh speeches in the source go
past 1992). The result is a coherent slice of the authoritarian-to-early-
democratic transition, all delivered as official presidential rhetoric.

## Slim / truncate rationale

- **Sample of 600**: stratified at **200 speeches per president** so that
  any Box Plot grouped by `president` compares equal-sized populations.
  All three presidents have at least 597 speeches in the source, so 200
  per president is well-balanced.
- **Truncate to 3,500 characters**: most speeches are short addresses or
  ribbon-cutting remarks (median ~2,200 characters), so very few are
  actually truncated. The cap mainly affects long policy speeches and New
  Year addresses. This keeps the file under the 5 MB hard cap.

## Columns

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `doc_id` | string | Synthetic ID, `authoritarian_speeches_0000` – `authoritarian_speeches_0599` |
| `text` | string | Cleaned speech body (Korean), truncated to 3,500 characters |
| `president` | categorical | English name: `Park Chung-hee` / `Chun Doo-hwan` / `Roh Tae-woo` (200 each) |
| `year` | integer | Speech year (1961–1992) |
| `date` | string (YYYY-MM-DD) | Speech date |
| `kind` | categorical | Speech type — original Korean labels (e.g. 신년사 New Year address, 연설 speech, 치사 commemorative address). See data dictionary for translations of common values. |
| `location` | string | Where the speech was delivered (Korean) |

## Suggested research questions

1. **Did regime sentiment soften across the three authoritarian
   presidents?** Apply the KNU sentiment dictionary, then use a Box Plot
   grouped by `president`. The democratic transition arc would predict
   Roh > Chun > Park in positive sentiment — but the official rhetoric
   may not match that pattern. (sentiment + Box Plot grouping)
2. **Do the three presidents have distinguishable rhetorical
   fingerprints?** Embed speeches with KLUE BERT, run k-means with k=3,
   then check whether the clusters align with the `president` labels. If
   they do, that's evidence each president had a recognisable rhetorical
   style; if they don't, official Blue House rhetoric may have a stable
   genre that overrides individual variation. (embeddings + clustering)
3. **What topics distinguish New Year addresses from commemorative speeches?**
   Filter to two `kind` values and fit a 6-topic LDA on the combined
   subset, then chart topic prevalence by `kind`. (LDA + grouping)
4. **Did 'security' / 'unification' rhetoric grow or shrink under the
   democratisation pressure of 1987-1992?** Fit an 8-topic LDA on the
   full 600 speeches, then plot topic prevalence by `year`. Look for
   shifts in the late-Chun and Roh years. (LDA + temporal trend)

## Reproducibility

Generated by `build.py` with `random_state=42`. Light cleaning only —
NFC Unicode normalisation, whitespace collapse, removal of zero-width
and BOM characters, removal of URLs and HTML entities, removal of
literal `\n` / `\r` / `\t` artefacts. **No Korean morphological
tokenisation** — students will do that in Orange via the Python Script
widget.

To rebuild from the upstream source:

```bash
cd private/week12/datasets/authoritarian_speeches
python3 build.py
```
